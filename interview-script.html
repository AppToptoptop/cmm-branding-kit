🎙 User Research

Interview Script

Structured guide for user discovery interviews. 15 questions across 6 themes. Target: people trying to lose weight or get fit.

20–25 min
Duration
15
Questions
6
Themes
25–45
Target Age
👋
Section 0
Introduction & Consent
2 min
📜 Script
« Thanks for taking the time! We're building a new type of coaching tool and we'd love to understand your experience with fitness, weight loss, and health apps. There are no right or wrong answers — we just want to hear your honest experience. This will take about 20 minutes. Everything is anonymous. Can I record this for my notes? »
⚠ Rules for the interviewer
• Never pitch the product during the interview
• Let silences breathe — the user will fill them
• Ask « why » and « tell me more » as much as possible
• Watch for emotional reactions: frustration, excitement, resignation
• Take notes on exact words they use (for copywriting later)
☕
Section 1
Ice Breaker & Context
3 min
Q1
How would you describe your relationship with fitness and health right now?
Open-ended warm up. Let them frame it in their own words. Listen for: frustration, guilt, motivation, resignation.
Q2
What's your current goal? Lose weight, get fit, or something specific like training for a race?
Maps to our 3 goal categories. Note which one they pick and how they describe it.
How long have you had this goal?
🔥
Section 2
Pain Points & Failures
5 min
Q3
What have you tried in the past to reach this goal?
List everything: apps, diets, coaches, gym memberships, challenges. Note what they tried first, how long each lasted.
Which one lasted the longest? Why did you eventually stop?
Q4
When you fall off track, what's usually the trigger?
Listen for: stress, social events, travel, boredom, lack of results, loneliness. This is the core insight we need.
What happens after you fall off? How do you feel?
Q5
Do you feel like you know WHAT to do, but the hard part is actually DOING it consistently?
Validates our core hypothesis: the problem isn't information, it's accountability. If they disagree, probe why.
📱
Section 3
Current Tools & Apps
4 min
Q6
What fitness or health apps do you currently have on your phone? How often do you actually open them?
Note the gap between installed apps and actual usage. Ask them to literally check their phone if comfortable.
Q7
Have you ever paid for a fitness app or coach? How much? Was it worth it?
Pricing signal. Note the exact amounts and what they considered « worth it » vs « a waste ».
What would make you pay for something like this again?
Q8
Do you track what you eat? If yes, how? If no, why not?
Validates photo food logging feature. Listen for: too tedious, forget, don't know portions, tried MyFitnessPal.
If you could just take a photo of your plate and get instant analysis, would that change things?
🤝
Section 4
Accountability & Social
4 min
Q9
Is there anyone in your life who keeps you accountable for your health goals?
Partner, friend, trainer, nobody. If nobody — that's our target user. If someone exists, ask how that dynamic works.
How does that feel when someone checks in on your progress?
Q10
Imagine receiving a daily message asking what you ate, if you worked out, and how you feel. Would that be motivating or annoying?
Core concept test. Watch their face carefully. Probe on the word they use: « annoying » vs « helpful » vs « depends ».
What would make it feel helpful rather than nagging?
Q11
If an AI coach contacted you daily, would you prefer a text message, a voice note, or an actual phone call?
Channel preference. Note their first instinct AND their reasoning. Ask about time of day too.
What time of day would work best for you?
💡
Section 5
Concept Reaction
4 min
📜 Brief the concept
« We're building an AI coaching assistant that contacts you every day via your preferred channel — text, voice note, or phone call. It tracks your food (you just send a photo), your exercise, and your mood. It holds you accountable, celebrates wins, and helps you get back on track when you slip. Think of it as a personal coach for €15/month instead of €300. »
Q12
What's your first reaction to that?
Capture the VERY FIRST thing they say. Don't prompt. Silence is ok. Their gut reaction is the most honest signal.
Q13
What would make you actually sign up for something like this?
Features, proof, testimonials, free trial? Note what they consider the « tipping point ».
What would be a dealbreaker?
💰
Section 6
Pricing & Close
3 min
Q14
If this existed today and it actually worked, what would you be willing to pay per month?
Don't anchor with our price. Let them name a number first. Then probe: « Would you still say yes at €15/month? What about €25? »
Would you prefer monthly or annual billing?
Q15
Is there anything we haven't talked about that you think is important for a tool like this?
Open-ended close. Often the best insights come from this question. Let them talk freely.
📜 Closing script
« This was incredibly helpful, thank you! We're launching a beta soon. Would you be interested in being one of our first testers? If yes, I'll add you to our waitlist. We'll also send you a small thank-you for your time. »

→ Collect: email, preferred channel, WhatsApp (if willing)
✅
After the interview
Post-Interview Checklist
Write summary within 30 minutes (memory is freshest)
Note the user's exact words for key emotions/pain points
Rate willingness to pay: 1 (no way) to 5 (shut up and take my money)
Rate channel preference: Message / Voice / Call
Capture the #1 pain point in one sentence
Note any feature ideas they mentioned
Add to waitlist if they opted in
Send thank-you message within 24 hours
